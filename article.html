Here’s give and take in any relationship, and that can apply to finances, too. Sure, you’re using his internet, and maybe even hiking up his electric bill a bit. On the other hand, you’re presumably spending money on gas or public transit to get to his place all the time. And if you’re paying rent for an apartment you rarely use, that’s an expense to consider, too.

My point is, you can play quid pro quo with these expenses all day long and calculate whether or not you’re contributing enough, or you can just have a conversation about it, which is a better idea. Most of the time, couples only talk about money when they’re fighting about it, so it’s good to start the discussion before that point. In your case, you can initiate this conversation in a totally natural way: Try something like, “Hey, I want to make sure I’m paying my fair share since I’m here all the time. Mind if we sit down and talk money?”
